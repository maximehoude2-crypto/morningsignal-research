Constellation Brands (STZ) — Q4 & Full-Year FY2026 | Released AMC April 8, Call at 8:00 AM ET April 9

Market Cap: ~$28B

What they reported: - Comparable EPS: $1.90 vs. $1.74 consensus — beat by $0.16 (9.2%) - GAAP EPS: $1.16 vs. $3.29 est — miss driven by one-time charges and impairments related to Wine & Spirits divestitures (SVEDKA) - Q4 Net Sales: $1.92B vs. ~$1.90B consensus — modest beat - Full-Year FY2026: Net sales $9.14B, comparable EPS $11.82, GAAP EPS $9.61 - Q4 Net Income: $224.7M vs. a loss of $370.6M a year ago

Segment breakdown: - Beer: Remained the #1 dollar share gainer across all U.S. tracked channels. Q4 beer sales ~$1.73B (+1% YoY). Shipment volumes +1.1%. Pacifico and Victoria posted double-digit depletion growth. Full-year beer depletions declined 2.1%, but that masks brand-level divergence: the Mexican lager portfolio (Modelo Especial, Corona, Pacifico) continues to take shelf space. - Wine & Spirits: Sales collapsed 58% to $194.2M on a 72.9% volume decline — but this is almost entirely the SVEDKA vodka divestiture and the 2025 wine portfolio exits. This is an intentional shrink, not demand destruction.

What management said that matters: - Demand described as "subdued" across categories — consumers trading down to cheaper beer and wine. - FY2027 comparable EPS guidance: $11.20–$11.90 vs. $12.36 consensus — a 7–9% miss to Street expectations. - Withdrew previously issued FY2028 outlook entirely, citing macroeconomic uncertainty. This is the headline. - Raised the quarterly dividend, signaling confidence in cash generation even as growth visibility deteriorates. - Incoming CEO Nicholas Fink joining the call — leadership transition underway.

Stock reaction: - Fell ~2% in after-hours trading to $147.26 on the guidance miss and FY28 withdrawal. - Pre-market April 9: recovered, up ~$2.48 to ~$154, suggesting the market is digesting the Q4 beat and dividend raise as partially offsetting the forward weakness. - UBS raised its price target from $168 to $176 and maintained a "buy" rating.

The one thing to watch: Beer depletion trends in Q1 FY2027. The 2.1% annual decline masks the brand-mix story — if Modelo/Pacifico can hold positive depletions while Corona stabilizes, the beer business is fine. If total beer depletions accelerate downward, the "trade-down" narrative becomes a structural problem.

Directional call: This print is fine but not inspiring. The Q4 beat shows execution, but management pulling FY28 guidance is a confidence signal — they don't have visibility, and they're telling you that. The stock is trading at ~13x forward EPS, which is cheap for a branded CPG company, but only if beer volume stabilizes. The dividend raise is a floor, not a catalyst. Hold. Wait for Q1 FY2027 beer data before getting constructive.
