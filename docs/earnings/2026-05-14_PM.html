Earnings Deep Dive — 2026-05-14 Post-Close

Executive Summary

Technology led Thursday’s session at +1.50%, while Materials lagged at -0.75% and SLV fell 4.84%, setting the day’s thematic tone. The broad tape was constructive, with SPY +0.79%, QQQ +0.71%, IWM +0.63%, and DIA +0.74%. That backdrop matters for tonight’s earnings slate: the strongest evidence clustered around tech and selective growth, while commodity-linked and pre-profit stories still faced a higher bar.

The most important post-close signal came from Applied Materials (AMAT). The supplied actuals show an EPS beat to $2.86 vs. $2.68 estimate, plus strong profitability with 48.99% gross margin and 29.89% operating margin. That said, the supplied revenue comparison is not fully clean: yfinance lists $7.01B revenue against an $8.15B estimate, while a same-day Yahoo headline says AMAT “surpasses Q2 earnings and revenue estimates.” The margin profile and AI-infrastructure framing are clearly constructive; the precise topline beat/miss framing needs a cleaner source.

Elsewhere, the slate split into two groups. On the cleaner side, Boot Barn (BOOT) posted a straightforward-looking beat on both EPS and revenue with healthy margins, and dLocal (DLO) delivered a modest revenue beat while remaining profitable. On the more mixed side, Figma (FIG) had guidance-positive headlines — including a raised annual revenue forecast tied to AI-driven demand — but the supplied actuals still show a very high gross margin (82.12%) alongside deep operating and net losses, underscoring that growth and profitability remain separate debates.

Healthcare produced the most fundamental thesis changes. Celcuity (CELC) remains pre-revenue, but its earnings transcript included substantive clinical takeaways: the company reported statistically significant and clinically meaningful progression-free-survival improvement in a key VIKTORIA-2 cohort, with manageable safety and no new safety signal flagged in the supplied excerpt. Heartflow (HTFL) also stood out because the headline explicitly says it raised full-year 2026 guidance, even though the quarter’s revenue came in roughly around consensus.

The rest of the tape reinforced a familiar pattern: small-cap quantum and frontier-tech names can show real revenue traction off small bases, but profitability remains far away. Xanadu (XNDU) and Infleqtion (INFQ) both showed topline momentum, yet losses remain large relative to revenue. In materials and mining, the session’s tape mattered: TMC and Americas Gold & Silver (USAS) remain more dependent on project execution, production, permitting, and commodity conditions than on quarterly P&L optics. Across multiple names, one caution is warranted: several supplied estimate fields appear noisy or basis-mismatched versus headlines, especially for ADRs and non-U.S. reporters.
