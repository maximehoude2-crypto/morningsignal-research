AMC Catch-Up: Thursday, April 9 After Close

WD-40 Company (WDFC) — Q2 FY2026 (Calendar Q1)

Market Cap: ~$3.0B | Sector: Consumer Staples (Household Products)

The Print:

  • Revenue: $161.7M vs. $154.9M estimate — beat by 4.4%. Sales rose 10.7% year over year.
  • Adjusted EPS: $1.32 vs. $1.40 estimate — missed by 5.7%. Profit of $20.3M for the quarter.

Segment Breakdown:

  • Americas (44% of net sales): $71.8M, up 10% YoY. U.S. maintenance product sales surged 15.4%, the standout metric in the quarter.
  • Gross margin improved 100 basis points year over year, driven by lower specialty chemical input costs and price increases flowing through.

Management Commentary:

  • WD-40 reaffirmed FY2026 guidance on a pro forma basis (excluding divested homecare/cleaning products): revenue of $630M–$655M and EPS of $5.75–$6.15.
  • However, the full-year EPS guidance range came in slightly below consensus, and the revenue guidance midpoint underwhelmed, creating the "beat on revenue, miss on EPS, guide soft" trifecta that the market punishes.

Stock Reaction:

  • WDFC was essentially flat to slightly down after hours, declining ~0.6% to $218 after closing the regular session at $221.75 (+1.6% on the day). The muted reaction reflects the offsetting dynamics: strong top-line growth but cost pressures eating into margins.

The One Thing to Watch:

  • Input cost trajectory. The 100bps gross margin improvement is encouraging, but specialty chemical costs remain volatile with the Middle East conflict ongoing. If crude-linked input costs re-accelerate, the EPS guidance range becomes harder to hold at the low end.

Directional Call:

  • This is a "hold and monitor" print. The top-line engine (15.4% U.S. maintenance growth) is firing, and the brand franchise remains strong. But the market needs to see margin expansion translate to EPS beats, not just revenue beats. Until cost headwinds abate, WDFC is a quality compounder in a holding pattern.
