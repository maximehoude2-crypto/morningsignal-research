A second cluster matters less for the quarter itself and more for forward demand. Forgent Power Solutions (FPS) said it raised fiscal 2026 guidance on record orders, backlog, and sequential margin expansion. Legence (LGN) was also framed by headlines as a beat with raised guidance, and Intuitive Machines (LUNR) highlighted a record quarter-end backlog of $1.1B plus positive adjusted EBITDA. Ondas (ONDS) raised its full-year 2026 revenue forecast to $390M. These are important signals for data-center infrastructure, electrification, defense-tech, drone, and space-execution baskets even where the raw yfinance estimate comparisons look messy.

That messiness is the biggest caution in today’s tape. Several names show clear source inconsistencies between the yfinance actual/estimate lines and the accompanying headlines or article framing: BN, VIK, LGN, KLAR, LUNR, YETI, and BLSH stand out. Examples: Klarna is headlined as having a “strong start” with $1Bn revenue and $68M adjusted operating profit, while the supplied yfinance actuals show $823M revenue and an operating loss. YETI has a detailed article with $380.4M revenue, while yfinance lists $583.7M. Viking has a headline saying sales beat estimates, while the supplied revenue line shows $1.72B vs. $2.14B estimate. So the right way to read this morning is not through mechanical beat/miss screens alone.

On revenue quality and margin structure, the winners were clear. Aya Gold & Silver (AYA) reported record revenue and cash flow with a 56.6% gross margin and 48.3% operating margin. VSNT, NIQ, and CLBT all showed strong margin profiles. By contrast, some thematic stories still need more proof. Bitdeer (BTDR) beat on revenue but had only a 4.7% gross margin and a deeply negative operating margin. Fermi (FRMI) reported zero revenue and a $133.2M net loss. Bullish (BLSH) has the noisiest print of the set, with a huge reported revenue figure that appears non-comparable alongside negative gross profit and a large net loss.

One final limitation: none of the 16 companies has conference-call or transcript evidence in the input set. So this is a press-release/news-flow read, not a post-call management deep dive. Where the quarter is clean, the take is high-confidence; where the figures conflict, the right label is caution until transcripts, filings, or fuller official tables are available.
